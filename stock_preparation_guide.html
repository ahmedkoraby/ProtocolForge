Step 3: Downstream Flexibility (from these stocks)

Option A: 50 µM Top (your current plan)

Example with AO-c284 (21.8 mM stock):

  • Dilute 21.8 mM stock ~430-fold to get 50 µM working solution
  • For 1 mL working soln: 2.3 µL stock + 180 µL DMSO + 818 µL media
  • Then 3-fold serial dilutions: 50 µM → 16.7 µM → 5.6 µM → ... → 23 nM

Option B: 100 nM Top (if EC50 is nM-potency)

Example with AO-c284 (21.8 mM stock):

  • Dilute 21.8 mM stock ~218,000-fold to get 100 nM working solution
  • This requires intermediate dilution steps (can't do directly)
  • Better: Make intermediate 10 µM stock first, then dilute to 100 nM
  • Then 3-fold serial dilutions: 100 nM → 33 nM → 11 nM → ... → 0.046 nM

Option C: Flexible "Scout" Range (to find real EC50)

Skip intermediate dilutions; test directly from stock:

  • Test 1–2 compounds at 10 single-point concentrations
  • Use stock at different 1:10, 1:100, 1:1000, 1:10,000 etc. dilution factors
  • See where activity appears — tells you EC50 ballpark
  • Then design Plates 1–3 with appropriate top/bottom range
Why These Stock Concentrations?
The 5–25 mM sweet spot:
  • High enough: Allows reasonable dilutions downstream without working with tiny pipette volumes (<1 µL is unreliable)
  • Low enough: Unlikely to precipitate when diluted into aqueous media or mixed with cells
  • Practical: 1 mL stocks are standard tube size, easy to label and store at 4°C long-term (in DMSO, stable for months)
  • Flexible: Can make both 50 µM and 100 nM working solutions from the same stock via intermediate dilutions
